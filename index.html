About Imam Hussain (as)

The beloved grandson of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), a beacon of justice and truth

The Legacy of Courage

Imam Hussain (as), the third Imam of the Shia Muslims and the grandson of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), stands as one of the most revered figures in Islamic history. His unwavering commitment to justice, truth, and moral principles has inspired millions across centuries.

Born in Medina, Imam Hussain was raised in the household of the Prophet, surrounded by divine guidance and prophetic wisdom. His character was molded by the teachings of his grandfather and the love of his father, Imam Ali (as), and mother, Fatimah (as).

The Man of Principles

Throughout his life, Imam Hussain demonstrated exceptional moral courage, choosing righteousness over worldly power. His refusal to pledge allegiance to tyranny and his stand against oppression at Karbala exemplify the highest principles of faith and humanity.

His message transcends religious boundaries, speaking to all humanity about the importance of standing up for justice, protecting the oppressed, and never compromising on fundamental human values.

The Battle of Karbala

The defining moment that changed the course of Islamic history

The Journey to Karbala

In 680 CE (61 AH), Imam Hussain embarked on a journey that would become one of the most significant events in Islamic history. Refusing to pledge allegiance to the Umayyad Caliph Yazid, whose rule he considered unjust and contrary to Islamic principles, Imam Hussain set out from Medina with his family and companions.

The Siege and Sacrifice

At the plains of Karbala in Iraq, Imam Hussain and his small band of 72 companions faced an army of thousands. Despite being vastly outnumbered and surrounded, they refused to surrender their principles. The tragic events of the 10th of Muharram (Ashura) saw the martyrdom of Imam Hussain and his companions, but their sacrifice illuminated the path of truth and justice for future generations.

The Message of Karbala

The tragedy of Karbala was not merely a political conflict, but a struggle between truth and falsehood, justice and oppression. Imam Hussain's sacrifice teaches us that some principles are worth dying for, and that standing up against tyranny is a moral obligation, regardless of the consequences.

His final words and actions continue to inspire people of all backgrounds to choose righteousness over convenience, truth over power, and justice over personal gain.

Historical Timeline

Key events in the life of Imam Hussain (as) and the tragedy of Karbala

Born in Medina as the younger son of Imam Ali and Fatimah, grandson of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

632 CE

Death of Prophet Muhammad

The Prophet passes away, leaving behind his teachings and the Quran as guidance for humanity

661 CE

Martyrdom of Imam Ali

Imam Ali, the father of Hussain and fourth Caliph, is martyred in Kufa

670 CE

Death of Imam Hassan

Imam Hassan, the elder brother, passes away under suspicious circumstances

680 CE

Journey to Karbala

Imam Hussain leaves Medina and travels toward Kufa, ultimately reaching Karbala

10 Muharram 61 AH

Day of Ashura

The tragic battle of Karbala occurs, resulting in the martyrdom of Imam Hussain and his companions
